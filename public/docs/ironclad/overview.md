# Ironclad — Character Overview

The **Ironclad** is a tanky frontline fighter who thrives on **Strength scaling** and aggressive self-healing. He has the highest starting HP in the game and a kit built around wearing enemies down while recovering from damage in the process.

## Playstyle

The Ironclad rewards going on the offensive. Many of his most powerful cards only work when you're leaning into dealing damage — which feeds his healing, which lets you survive long enough to snowball. He rarely wins through pure defense alone.

His core game plan shifts depending on which build you commit to early, but every run benefits from:

- **At least one Strength source** — even in defensive builds, Strength multiplies your already-large hit numbers.
- **An Exhaust synergy** — the Ironclad has more Exhaust cards than any other character, and many of his best relics and cards trigger when you Exhaust.
- **Knowing when to take damage** — his starting relic *Burning Blood* heals 6 HP after every combat, which turns small amounts of chip damage into a non-issue if you play efficiently.

## Strengths

| Strength | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|
| High base HP (80) | Survives mistakes and elite fights that would cripple other characters |
| Strong AoE options | Whirlwind and Cleave keep Act 1 mobs manageable |
| Reliable Strength scaling | Multiple cards and relics to stack Strength quickly |
| Built-in sustain | Burning Blood + Reaper + Feed make HP a renewable resource |

## Weaknesses

- **Weak early draw** — his deck naturally runs lean on card draw, so cycling to key cards can feel slow without help from relics or specific cards.
- **Vulnerable to debuffs** — Frail and Weak hit him harder than other characters because they blunt the big damage numbers he relies on.
- **No native energy generation** — unlike the Defect, the Ironclad can't easily generate bonus energy. Energy-hungry builds need to plan around this.

## Early Priority Picks

These cards are almost always worth taking in Act 1:

- **Inflame** — +2 Strength permanently is value you can't ignore.
- **Armaments** — Upgrade a card. A+ tier if you have several damage cards that benefit heavily from upgrading.
- **Pommel Strike** — A Strike that draws a card is a two-for-one that keeps your deck moving.
- **Shrug It Off** — Cheap block with conditional upside. A defensive anchor in the early game.
- **Spot Weakness** — Free Strength against elites. Can single-handedly carry an Ironclad run.

## Tips

> **Don't skip Defend cards too aggressively.** The Ironclad is tough, but Act 2 and Act 3 enemies hit hard enough that a total lack of block will drain even his large HP pool. You want at least a few block sources before entering Act 2.

> **Burning Blood scales with run length.** Every combat you survive with low HP is 6 HP back in your pocket. In long runs or high-ascension levels, this quietly adds up to hundreds of points of healing.
