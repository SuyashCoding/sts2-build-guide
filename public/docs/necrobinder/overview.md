# Necrobinder — Character Overview

The **Necrobinder** is a summoner and life-drain specialist who controls the battlefield by raising undead minions, siphoning HP from enemies, and converting death into power. She has moderate starting HP (72) but an unmatched ability to sustain herself — and her army — through the thick of even the hardest fights.

## Playstyle

Where most characters fight alone, the Necrobinder fights with a **field of minions** who absorb damage, deal chip damage, and die gloriously to fuel her abilities. Her starting relic *Soulbound Phylactery* rewards this cycle: every minion death makes her stronger.

Managing your minion field is the core loop. You want bodies on the field to absorb hits, just enough HP to survive without them, and the Drain spells to heal back when things get thin.

## Strengths

| Strength | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|
| Strong field presence | Minions force enemies to divide their attention |
| Excellent sustain | Drain spells make HP a near-renewable resource in long fights |
| Snowball potential | Minion deaths compound into Strength gains that accelerate each wave |
| Unique risk/reward | Intentionally sacrificing minions at key moments triggers some of the strongest effects in her kit |

## Weaknesses

- **Dependent on minion availability** — if minions die too quickly without replacement, you're left exposed with no board.
- **Energy hungry** — summoning minions costs energy, and maintaining a full field can drain your hand quickly.
- **Struggles against multi-hit attacks** — AoE damage wipes your minion field in one turn, leaving you suddenly vulnerable.

## Tips

> **Don't be afraid to let minions die.** The Phylactery exists precisely because minion death is part of the game plan, not a failure. Sacrifice them intentionally at the right moment for maximum Strength.

> **Drain spells scale with your max HP, not your current HP.** Building a higher HP ceiling makes each drain return more value per cast.
