# Silent — Poison Build Guide

Poison Silent revolves around the player dealing a significant amount of Damage over time, ramping up as you play more poison cards that chip away at the enemy's health. The amount of Damage done to an enemy is equivalent to the amount of poison stacked on them, which typically decreases by 1 at the end of each turn, seen as **Decay**. Poison bypasses Block when dealing Damage to an enemy's health.

- **Best Offense is a Good Defense:** Since chip damage is your main form of attack, defensive cards to block incoming attacks helps keep you alive while your damage-over-time strategy takes down your opponent.
- **Poison Power:** Make sure to keep using low cost cards to keep your opponent poisoned so their health steadily drains over the course of the battle.
- **Maximizing Multipliers:** Once poison is applied and you have a safe position, utilize cards that enhance or multiply the amount of poison damage to end the fight quicker.

## What to Aim For

The most important thing in your run is to stack as much Poison as possible on your enemies and build up your Block counter to protect yourself as chip damage wins you the match. Upgrading your Poison cards and especially the ones that multiply Poison damage are a must, Accelerant which helps the Poison counter build up on enemies instead of staying the same when upgraded.

## Most Relevant Cards

The following list contains a selection of some of the best cards for a Poison Silent deck ranked on card rarity.

### Common

- **Cloak and Dagger** - Gain 6 Block, add a Shiv to your hand.
- **Dagger Spray** - Deal 4 Damage to all enemies twice.
- **Deadly Poison** - Apply 5 Poison.
- **Poisoned Stab** - Deal 6 Damage and Apply 3-4 Poison.

### Uncommon

- **Bouncing Flask** - Apply 3 Poison to a random Enemy 3 times.
- **Blur** - Gain 5 Block, and your next Block is not removed at the start of next turn.
- **Bubble Bubble** - If Enemy is Poisoned, Apply 9 Poison.
- **Catalyst** - Doubles the Enemy's Exhaust and Poison.
- **Escape Plan** - Draw 1 Card, and if it's a Skill, Gain 3 Block.
- **Follow Through** - Deal 6 Damage to all enemies, and if last played card was a Skill, Apply 1 Weak to all enemies.
- **Footwork** - Gain 2 Dexterity.
- **Infinite Blades** - At the start of turn, add a Shiv to your hand.
- **Noxious Fumes** - At the start of turn, Apply Poison to all enemies.
- **Outbreak** - Every 3 times you Apply Poison, deal 11 Damage to all enemies.
- **Pinpoint** - Deal 15 Damage, costs 1 less energy for every Skill played on current turn.

### Rare

- **Accelerant** - Whenever Poison deals Damage, Poison Damage Value stays the same instead of decreasing by 1 every turn.
- **Burst** - During this turn, your next Skill is played twice.
- **Echoing Slash** - Deals 10 Damage to all enemies, repeating whenever an enemy dies.
- **Envenom** - When an attack deals Unblocked Damage, Apply 1 Poison.

## Overall Attack

When playing a Poison Silent Build, your attack boils down to inflict Poison, build Block, Apply Debuff, and repeat for your gameplay loop, so boss breakdowns are going to look into whether you need to Stall your enemy, Race to kill them before they crush you, if they have a Setup Window for you to stack Poison, or if they have a straight up Poison weakness and are fairgame.

---

## Act 1

For Act 1, your main priority should be building up your Poison and Defense cards for the rest of the game. It is vital to focus on grabbing cards that inflict Poison first as that will be your main form of attack, then defense cards, and then cards that capitalize upon pre-existing Poison in your enemies. Once you have a decent deck, try taking on paths that lead you to Uncommon and Rare cards so you have a chance to start looking for your heavy-hitting damage cards for later in the run. Relics like Snecko Skull for extra Poison application, Twisted Funnel for AOE Poison at the start of combat, and Unsettling Lamp that doubles the first debuff card's effect at the start of battle are good to keep an eye out for when dealing with Elites. The Specimen, a Rare Relic, is a key part of the build if you can find it as it helps your Damage by transferring leftover Poison off a dying enemy onto another to bring them down even faster.

### Overgrowth Elites

- **Byrdonis** - Strength increases over time, plan is to Poison, Apply Debuffs and Gain Block while playing the long game.
- **Phrog Parasite** - Punishes a slow start, so focus on Applying Poison and only Gaining Block when needed, don't stall but play safe and slow.
- **Bygone Effigy** - Has a setup window but becomes dangerous after setup, abuse the setup window and if you have Catalyst, use it to spike Damage.

### Overgrowth Bosses

- **Ceremonial Beast** - You can see their hits coming, and poison is really effective here, just make sure you Gain Block to deal with incoming attacks, be careful as you can only play one card at a time.
- **Kin Priest** - You need to hurry before they crush you with their buffs and heals, stack Poison fast.
- **Vantom** - Poison dominates the fight, so just keep on stacking it and its a free win.

### Underdocks Elites

- **Shulking Colony** - Noxious Fumes is great, make sure to spread Poison out, don't focus on single targets if you can.
- **Phantasmal Gardner** - Stall, play safe while making sure Poison does chip damage and don't lose your hand too early.
- **Terror Eel** - They've got high burst damage, Apply Weak and Poison while keeping Block up and you're golden.

### Underdocks Bosses

- **Waterfall Giant** - Stall these guys, they've got pretty clear attack patterns so Poison when safe and Block on attack turns.
- **Soul Fysh** - Poison early fast and hard, because they will outpace you with healing if you don't.
- **Lagavulin Matriarch** - Utilize the setup window, and then focus on defense when they start getting aggressive while Poison wins you the fight.

---

## Act 2

You should have a pretty decent hand and a couple of artifacts at this point. This should be when you get the final missing pieces for your build and sweep the game. The difficulty does spike a bit, but remember that any Elites you battle may drop the Relic or Card that may boost you over the finish line. Unless you're doing pretty bad damage wise, most Common and some Uncommon cards may not be worth your time to keep collecting.

### Act 2 Elites

- **Infested Prism** - Stall, deal with simple attack patterns, and let Poison win.
- **Decimillipede** - Revives segments, so AOE Poison helps win the fight.
- **Entomancer** - Poison abuse boss, go to town with Poison stacking and you should be clear for the win.
- **Hunter Killer** - Play fewer cards with more damage so the Hunter Killer can't discount as much Damage.
- **Kaiser Crab** - Poison bypass armor, so this is a pretty easy fight all things considered as long as you stall.
- **The Insatiable** - Race with Poison and do as much damage before the timer runs out.
- **Knowledge Demon** - Use the Setup window to promote Poison and then stall as long as possible.

---

## Act 3

If you've made it to Act 3, you're doing fine and should be just about ready to beat the game. This should be a piece of cake if all goes well, though picking up a Rare Card or 2 could always make it a bit easier. Good luck on the final stretch and all hope that bad RNG doesn't mess up your run.

### Act 3 Elites

- **Knights** - Stall, and focus on Block as AOE Poison takes down your enemies.
- **Mecha Knight** - Stall, scale your Poison and use your burst cards to expedite the Damage done before they take you down, watch out for big telegraphed hits.
- **Soul Nexus** - Stall, just focus on Block and Poison and you'll be fine.

### Act 3 Bosses

- **The Doormaker** - Apply steady amounts of Poison early and focus on Block during the fight while slowly scaling Poison.
- **Test Subject** - Stall and keep in control, don't spam any unnecessary skills, just focus on Poison and Block like always.
- **Queen** - Final Battle Time. Queen starts by Applying 3 Chains of Binding to the first three cards you draw. Bind lets you only play 1 Bound card on the current turn, before being unbound on the next. Queen then Applies 99 Frail, Weak, and Vulnerable on the player. Queen then alternates Applying 1 Strength to all current allies, and Gaining 20 Block until the end of the fight. Torch Head Amalgam does a big attack 3 turns in a row, then performing a multishot attack, before restarting its attack pattern. When Torch Head is defeated, Queen switches her turn to Gain 2 Strength. She then attacks in a cycle. Poison will eat through her Block, so focus on staying alive by stalling and you'll be golden.
