# Silent — Discard Overview

Discard Silent aims to draw deep with cards like **Accelerate** and **Prepared**, then discard **Sly** cards in order to play them for free. At its apex, you will be able to comfortably one round anything the game could possibly throw at you. Even if you aren't going for a full discard build, the draw cards are generically strong.

## What To Aim For

In magical cupcake fantasy land, you would have a ~15 size deck, enough copies of **Tactician** and Draw/Discard cards to generate infinite amounts of energy, and **Corrosive Wave** always active. This will almost never happen in any non-seeded run. However, getting close is often more than enough to win your run. Here are some general principles to go by:

- Upgrade **[Neutralize](https://slaythespire.wiki.gg/wiki/Slay_the_Spire_2:Neutralize)** First - Neutralize is an invaluable asset for early game survivability. You will be able to keep enemies weakened most, if not all, of the time.

- **If it gives Energy, take it** - Energy is your biggest limiter in STS2. Relics like **Chandelier** and **Super Hot Cocoa**, as well as cards like **Tactician**, are must-takes.

- **Keep your deck small** - Keeping your deck as small as you can is universally good for every character, but goes double for the Silent. Our end goal is to draw through our whole deck several times in one turn. Powers can be a way to cheat a small deck, as once they are played once they are out of your deck.

## Most Relevant cards

This is a list of the cards I would consider taking if offered, in order of highest priority to lowest priority, along with some reasoning as to why. This list isn't a hard rule and will depend on the state of your run.

- **Corrosive Wave** - By far the best win condition for this style of Silent. If you can keep drawing, you can stack infinite poison.

- **Tactician** - Repeatable energy generation. Great to get at any point of the game; even early on it can be triggered with Survivor.

- **Adrenaline** - Gives you energy and draws cards. Great at any point.

- **Accelerant** - Only if you already have *Corrosive Wave* or large amounts of poison otherwise.

- **Master Planner** - Largely useful earlier on but has applications late game as well. Try to upgrade it to reduce its cost.

- **Calculated Gamble** - Disgusting with *Corrosive Wave*, triggers all of your Slys and gives you a fresh hand.

- **Acrobatics** - Draws the deepest and gives you a discard on top.

- **Prepared** - Good unupgraded but becomes fantastic when upgraded. Prepared+ > Acrobatics.

- **Reflex** - More card draw, useful at any point, but only take mid-late game with sufficient discard.

- **Tools Of The Trade** - Great early to mid game as well as some late game scenarios. If you aren't going through multiple turns late game then don't take it.

- **Stratagem** - The only colorless card I will put here just because it is superb to have if you are already off to the races. Unlikely to find.

- **Escape Plan** - The block rarely comes up; it's more useful as a 0 cost deck thinner.

## Act 1

Your main goal in Act 1 is to set yourself up for success. Plan your route in order to take as many elite fights as possible and try to increase your average damage as much as you can. The best way to do this is by removing/transforming Strikes whenever you can and replacing them with cards that do more damage or have more utility. Cards like **Footwork** and **Piercing Wail** can help you get through the early hallway fights while taking minimal damage. You should almost always prioritize taking as little damage as possible rather than killing an enemy quickly at the expense of health, as your healing is limited. Elites and bosses may break this rule.

## Overgrowth Elites

- **Brydonis** - In contention for the easiest Act 1 Elite in the game. Brydonis gains 1 Strength every turn, so you shouldn't keep the fight going for too long, but you have a lot of time.

- **Phrog Parasite** - Phrog will try to fill your deck with **Infection** as the fight goes on. The Silent is one of the better characters at dealing with it due to being able to discard them, but you shouldn't get too lackadaisical. Upon dying, Phrog will create 4 Wrigglers that will be stunned for their first turn. If possible, try to kill Phrog with poison; doing so will grant you 2 free turns with which to handle the Wrigglers.

- **Bygone Effigy** - Effigy can be a run killer if encountered early and/or with a sequence of bad draws. He will give you 2 free turns with which you should throw as much damage at him as possible. After these turns, he'll start hitting you for 25 damage per turn every turn. This damage isn't something you can consistently block early game, so focus on killing him as fast as you can even if you need to eat damage. Use your highest-damage cards last.

## Overgrowth Bosses

- **Ceremonial Beast** - Ceremonial Beast is like Terror Eel with a bit more depth to its game. Your first turn is free, so spend it doing whatever damage you can and setting up powers. The boss will simultaneously attack and increase its own Strength most turns, but its Strength will be reset and it will be stunned upon reaching the health specified by Plow. In between the beast's assaults, it will apply a unique debuff to you called **Ringing**. For the turn it is in effect, the player can only play 1 card that turn. The best ways to handle this are setting up **Blur** and/or a **Dodge and Roll** on the debuff turn to try to mitigate the damage. Alternatively, if you can stun it on the Ringing turn or the debuff turn, that works as well.

- **Kin Priest** - The Kin Priest fight consists of the Priest themselves and 2 Kin Followers. The followers themselves aren't particularly deadly, but you should still remove them first to stop their damage. AOE will serve you well here. The Priest itself does more damage than the followers, but still middling compared to the kind of hits Elites and other Bosses throw around. The more annoying part is that the Priest will inflict the player with **Frail** first and then **Weak** the turn after.

- **Vantom** - Among the Overgrowth bosses, Vantom is the hardest nut to crack for us, though it pales in comparison to the Underdocks bosses. The first thing you'll notice about Vantom is his unique power, **Slippery**. Slippery reduces all of the first 9 instances of damage he takes to 1. This notably includes poison damage. If you see Vantom is your boss, aim to get at least one copy of **Ricochet** to burst through his Slippery as quickly as you can. Vantom's hits themselves increase in damage every turn until the third, where it does maximum damage. If possible, try to set yourself up with **Blur** or **Dodge and Roll** to help mitigate the damage.

## Underdocks Elites

- **Skulking Colony** - You should aim to reach the 20 damage limit, or get as close as possible, to kill Colony before he fills your deck with too many Dazes.

- **Phantasmal Gardener** - Your ability to fight this elite depends heavily on your AOE options. You'll likely only be able to hit them once each turn, and you should try to use that hit on either an AOE strike or a stronger, focused attack to kill Gardeners one by one. **Ricochet** is very bad here; actively avoid discarding it if you have other hits to do. **Piercing Wail** can often stop you from taking any damage on a given turn.

- **Terror Eel** - True to its name, Terror Eel can be a pretty scary boss. It has the highest HP of any Act 1 elite, more comparable to the Act 1 boss. Its Achilles' heel is that it is stunned upon falling to 70 HP. Ideally, you can time this attack after Eel puts Vigor on himself. Also be aware that it will eventually apply permanent Vulnerability on you, increasing the damage you take by 50%.

## Underdocks Bosses

- **Waterfall Giant** - Waterfall Giant is a damage-check boss who will try to win by stalling you long enough for him to accumulate enough **Steam Eruption** to try to kill you in one blow. Not unlike many other bosses, the first turn is a setup turn for you. After that, the race against the clock begins. Waterfall Giant will inflict you with Weak and heal itself in order to try to slow you down long enough for it to accumulate more Steam Eruption. After you bring him down to 0, you have a free turn in order to try to block as much of the incoming damage as you can.

- **Soul Fysh** - Fysh is likely the most difficult boss for us in all of Act 1. Fysh, like Waterfall Giant, aims to stall you out. They do middling damage, but will slowly fill your deck with **Beckon**, the status card with the second highest damage dealt to you at turn end in the game. We already dislike anything that messes with our draws, but we can discard the Beckons for a time. What makes this fight particularly evil is that every fourth turn, Soul Fysh will give himself **Intangible** for 2 turns. Intangible reduces all damage the Fysh receives to 1, including poison damage. This means that after turn 4, you will not be able to damage Fysh for 40% of the fight. It is okay to be reckless in order to kill Fysh as soon as possible, and you should take the invulnerable turns as opportunities to try to block as much damage to yourself and apply as much poison to the Fysh as possible. Do not try to chip away at him during his invulnerable turns unless he is very, very low.

- **Lagavulin Matriarch** - Lagavulin Matriarch starts asleep and with 12 Plating. You have 3 turns to set up as much as you can, provided that Lagavulin doesn't take any unblocked damage. Notably, **This Includes Poison Damage**. If you poison the boss, they are guaranteed to wake up the next turn and start acting. Lagavulin makes up for the slow start with heavy damage and a brutal -2 Strength, -2 Dexterity alongside +2 Strength to itself every 4 turns. If you reach turn 8, your blocks will turn into bricks, as they will only block 1 damage per pop. Poison gets around Weakness, but you will struggle to stay alive long enough if it ever gets to that point.

## Act 2

By this point you should have a *concept* of a build and some relics to your name. Act 2 should be where you refine your build as necessary. You should still aim to take Elite fights where you can, the more the better, but be aware of the difficulty spike. You should stop taking early-game cards by this point unless you are still struggling on damage (ex. **Leading Strike**, **Strangle**, **Sucker Punch**).

## Warning

Your first 2 encounters in Act 2 are drawn from a weaker set of enemies. Try to avoid taking hallway fights after this point if you can. Among the more difficult hallway fights is the potential to run into **Hunter Killer**.

Hunter Killer might as well be an elite, but drops regular mob loot. They have 120 HP, putting them in Act 1 boss territory, and apply a unique status effect to you called **Tender**. Tender decreases your Strength and Dexterity by 1 for every action you take. In short, the more actions you take, the weaker each one becomes. This is a hard counter to our action-happy playstyle.

## Act 2 Elites

- **Infested Prism** - Infested Prism is a giant block of stats. He doesn't have too much going on; he just has a lot of HP and hits hard. In exchange for these stats, he has an ability where you regain 1 energy the first time you hit him in a turn. Make sure to take this into account.

- **Decimillipede** - Decimillipede is composed of 3 very similar enemies who need to be killed together within the span of 2 turns: the turn you kill them, followed by the turn they regenerate. AOE is obviously helpful, but if you are lacking it try to weaken them all to comparable levels, then knock one or two out and then knock out the third in a follow-up turn. Taking damage in this fight is almost always a given.

- **Entomancer** - Entomancer can either be an easy elite or a hellish one depending on your setup. While you can discard Dazes, you shouldn't allow Entomancer to fill your deck with them. Fortunately, the Silent comes equipped with poison, a way of dealing damage that gets around their gimmick. If you have a good amount of poison cards, this fight is easier. **Thorns** is also particularly effective here, as Entomancer attacks a lot of times in small amounts. Regardless, try to minimize the amount of times you hit him.

## Act 2 Bosses

- **Kaiser Crab** - The Kaiser Crab boss fight consists of two enemies, **Crusher** and **Rocket**, to your left and right respectively. The gimmick of this boss fight is that you take 50% more damage from the claw you are turned away from, and you can change which way you are facing by attacking one of the claws. Crusher is the lesser of the two, doing significantly less damage but applying Weak and Frail every so often. Rocket does significantly more damage, but has a recharge turn after he rocket claws you. It is still worth trying to keep both claws at similar HP, as if there is still one claw alive it will gain 99 Block and 5 Strength following the death of the other.

- **The Insatiable** - After you've mentally recovered from how ugly The Insatiable is, you can use your boss-mandated free setup turn to play your powers or get some early damage down. After that, The Insatiable will alternate between high-damage attacks and increasing its own Strength. While this is going on, you will be pulled closer to the Insatiable every turn. If your sandpit counter reaches 0, it is an instadeath regardless of how much block you may have. To delay this, you are given 6 copies of **Frantic Escape**, which will increase your sandpit counter by 1 at the expense of a 1 energy cost increase for that particular copy.

- **Knowledge Demon** - Knowledge Demon is the rarest Act 2 boss and also the most deadly. On the first, fifth, and ninth turns, Knowledge Demon will grant you the choice between 2 terrible curses. One of them is a damage curse and the other eats away at your ability to defeat the demon. The options are as follows:

  - **Set 1**: **Disintegration** (6 damage/turn) or **Mind Rot** (draw 1 fewer card/turn)
  - **Set 2**: **Disintegration** (7 damage/turn) or **Sloth** (max 3 cards/turn)
  - **Set 3**: **Disintegration** (8 damage/turn) or **Waste Away** (1 less Energy/turn)

  It is highly recommended that you take Mind Rot first, unless you are strong enough to where you can speed kill the demon in 2-3 turns. From there, you can make your own judgment based on the state of the fight and how much the debuffs will affect you.

## Act 3

Congratulations! You've made it to Act 3. If all has gone well enough, Act 3 should be a victory lap for your deck. By the time you reach Act 3, very few cards will make your deck better than it already is. Still, it's not too late to pick up a gamechanger to help put the cherry on top of your build. If you die here, it's quite likely that it is the result of some mistakes or poor luck earlier on in the run.

## Act 3 Elites

- **Knights** - The deadliest elite encounter in Act 3, in my opinion. Spectral Knight will turn your cards Ethereal and Magi Knight will strip your cards of their upgrades the following turns. Flail Knight... Flail Knight is also there. If you've been taking this guide's advice about making your deck as small as possible, you must must MUST kill Spectral Knight as soon as possible. Follow it up by killing Magi Knight to take your upgrades back, then put Flail Knight out of his misery.

- **Mecha Knight** - Mecha Knight is a pretty simple stat stick. Where their difficulty comes from is them putting 4 copies of Burn into your deck. Once again, you should have the draw and discard power to make it not too much of an issue unless it goes for too too long.

- **Soul Nexus** - Soul Nexus is another stat stick, but one that does a lot more damage. Fortunately, you can take as long as you like dealing with Soul Nexus. He does not increase his Strength, only applies Vulnerable and Weak to you for 2 turns. Ideally you can kill them before they get the drops off.

## Act 3 Bosses

- **The Doormaker** - The Doormaker has 2 forms: The door, and the maker himself. The door does quite a lot of damage, but only has 155 HP. When the door is defeated, the Doormaker will appear and be stunned. The maker himself does even more damage than the door and will stick around for 2 attacks before "closing" and buffing the door. Notably, if you inflict the Doormaker with poison and he escapes, he will return with the poison he left with upon the door opening again.

- **Test Subject** - Test Subject's fight happens in 3 phases. In the first phase, he will have 100 HP and gain Strength every time you play a skill. This is dangerous if you can't one round him, but you should be dealing 100 damage comfortably by this point. Subject will also lose all Strength obtained in this phase upon progressing to phase 2. In phase 2, Subject has 200 HP and adds Wounds to your deck every time he hits you. Subject's damage will ramp fast, as each turn he will increase the amount of hits by 1. In Phase 3, Subject will have 300 HP and becomes functionally immune to damage every other turn. He will also try to fill your deck with Burns Mecha Knight-style, which could be a problem if you took several Wound cards in Phase 2.

- **Queen** - If you rolled Queen as your Act 3 boss, I am sorry for your loss. Queen is by far the most difficult boss and it isn't even close. Queen gives you **Chains of Binding** on your first turn, which makes one of 3 cards that you draw unplayable. This isn't too bad for us, though certainly not ideal if your deck isn't that consistent. Turn 2 she then proceeds to apply 99 Frail, Weak, and Vulnerable. This is DEVASTATING. With Weak taken into account, she has more effective health than Doormaker and Subject, but also brings along a hard-hitting minion. Poison gets around Weakness, but Queen is a giant deck check for Silent. If you can kill her in 1-2 turns? GG. If you can't? This is going to be a very hard battle.
