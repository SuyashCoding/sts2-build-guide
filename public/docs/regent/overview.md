# Regent — Character Overview

The **Regent** is a wealthy, relic-obsessed ruler whose entire kit is built around one thing the other characters treat as a side effect: **Gold**. She starts with more gold than anyone else (150 vs. the standard 99), and her cards, relics, and abilities all find ways to turn that gold into raw power.

## Playstyle

The Regent plays differently from any other character because **Gold is both her resource and her weapon**. Some cards spend Gold for massive effects. Others generate Gold passively so you never run dry. Her starting relic *Golden Signet* drips 10 Gold per Act, ensuring even a cautious player who avoids shops and sacrifices is still accumulating the currency that powers her engine.

She rewards a particular kind of greed — knowing when to spend big for a decisive advantage versus when to hoard for a larger payoff later.

## Strengths

| Strength | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|
| Self-sufficient resource generation | Never fully runs out of Gold regardless of shop spending |
| Shop synergies are unmatched | Every other character treats shops as useful; she treats them as mandatory power spikes |
| Relic-heavy kit | Her cards interact with relics in ways that compound in unexpected directions |
| High ceiling | A perfectly optimised Regent run is among the most powerful in the entire game |

## Weaknesses

- **Early-game fragility** — until the Gold engine is running, she hits about as hard as a wet scroll.
- **Punished by early HP loss** — spending Gold aggressively means less reserve for healing at Campfires. Balance is essential.
- **Build variance** — the Gold build and the Relic build play like almost different characters. Committing to the wrong path mid-run can leave you stranded.

## Tips

> **Never skip a Shop floor if you can help it.** The Regent's power level is directly tied to how much she's been through shops. Even buying one card per shop snowballs into a huge advantage by Act 3.

> **The Golden Signet's 10 Gold per Act sounds small — it isn't.** Over three Acts that's 30 baseline Gold on top of combat drops, which is often the difference between affording a game-changing relic or going without.
